Most of the Universe is made of dark matter and dark energy, which cannot be directly observed. Dark matter adds gravity to galaxies, while dark energy drives the accelerated expansion of space.

The Universe is unimaginably vast, measured in light-years and containing more galaxies than we can see. Even the nearest stars are so far away that their light takes years to reach us.

The cosmic microwave background is faint radiation left over from the early Universe. It provides strong evidence for the Big Bang and helps scientists measure the Universes age and structure.
